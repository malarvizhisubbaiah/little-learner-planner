# Reading Curriculum — Ages 3–4

## Milestones

By age 4, children should be working toward:
- Recognizing their own name in print
- Holding a book correctly and turning pages
- Understanding that print carries meaning
- Retelling simple stories in their own words
- Identifying some letters (especially in their name)
- Enjoying being read to and asking for favorite books

## Weekly Focus Areas

### Weeks 1–4: Book Handling & Story Enjoyment
- How to hold a book, turn pages left to right
- Point to words as you read
- Talk about the cover — "What do you think this book is about?"
- Ask "What happened?" after reading

### Weeks 5–8: Print Awareness
- Letters make words, words make sentences
- Point to individual words while reading
- Find letters from the child's name in books
- "Can you find the letter S on this page?"

### Weeks 9–12: Story Comprehension
- Ask who, what, where questions after reading
- Retell the story using the pictures
- Predict what happens next
- Connect stories to real life — "Have you ever seen a cat like that?"

### Weeks 13–16: Vocabulary Building
- Introduce 2–3 new words per story
- Use new words in daily conversation
- Play word games — "Can you think of another word for happy?"
- Start a picture dictionary together

| Book | Author | Focus |
|------|--------|-------|
| The Very Hungry Caterpillar | Eric Carle | Counting, days of the week |
| Brown Bear, Brown Bear | Bill Martin Jr. | Repetition, colors, animals |
| Chicka Chicka Boom Boom | Bill Martin Jr. | Alphabet, letter recognition |
| Goodnight Moon | Margaret Wise Brown | Rhyming, bedtime routine |
| The Three Little Pigs | Traditional | Story structure, retelling |
| Green Eggs and Ham | Dr. Seuss | Rhyming, repetition |
| Where the Wild Things Are | Maurice Sendak | Imagination, emotions |

| Each Peach Pear Plum | Janet & Allan Ahlberg | Rhyming, I Spy |
| We're Going on a Bear Hunt | Michael Rosen | Repetition, sound words |

## Activity Ideas

1. **Story Walk** — Walk through the pictures before reading. "What do you see? What might happen?"
2. **Name Hunt** — Find letters from the child's name in books, signs, and cereal boxes
3. **Retell with Toys** — Act out a story using stuffed animals or figurines
4. **Make a Book** — Fold paper, draw pictures, and "write" a story together
5. **Library Trip** — Let the child choose their own books
